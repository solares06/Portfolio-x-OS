## Type is Felt Before It Is Read

Before a single word is processed, typography communicates. The weight of a heading, the spacing of a paragraph, the contrast between serif and sans-serif — all of these create an emotional landscape that the reader enters before conscious reading begins.

### The Art of Contrast

High-contrast typographic pairings create visual tension that energises a layout. A heavy, condensed display face paired with a light, generous body face creates a dialogue between strength and delicacy.

### Choosing Pairings

The best pairings share an underlying structural similarity while differing in expression:

- **Geometric sans + Humanist serif** — Modern clarity meets classical warmth
- **Condensed display + Extended body** — Vertical energy balanced by horizontal calm
- **Heavy weight + Light weight** — Visual anchor complemented by airy readability

### Practical Considerations

- Limit to 2 typefaces per project — 3 at most
- Establish a clear role for each: display, body, accent
- Test at multiple sizes — a pairing that works at 48px may fail at 14px
- Consider the emotional register: does this combination feel right for the content?

Typography is not a technical exercise. It is an act of empathy — anticipating how a reader will feel in the presence of your words.
